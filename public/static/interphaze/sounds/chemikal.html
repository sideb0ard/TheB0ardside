Mad props to the Glasgow label Chemikal Underground.
Being from Glasow, but now living in San Francisco, i 
still follow the label closely, and one of the main things 
i miss about Glasgow was the vibrant local music scene,
which was helped greatly by the DIY attitude of Chemikal.
The label was created by the newly formed band The Delgados 
in 1994. Full of energy and youthful optimism, they stuck a 
finger up to the London record labels that were the status 
quo at the time with their "Come to us and play our game"
attitude, the gleswegian foursome decided to form their own 
label so they could do things their own way.

I can't remember the first time i saw the delgados play, but 
i remember being stoned in my room, dancing around listening 
the "Monica Webster" 7" playing at full volume. That was 
their first label release, and i was just so excited by the 
sound and so happy that there was such good music coming from 
glasgow, and more precisely from Motherwell, Lanarkshire, 
only two miles from my own home town! As it turns out, the 
drummer Paul Savage was next door neighbour to my room 
mate when he was a kid. doh!
Anyway, their second release was by another as then unheard 
of group named Bis, full of attitude and punk pop heaven. 
Bis were looking for a label at the time, and Chemikal 
promised to release their single straight away, 
'Disco Nation 45'.
